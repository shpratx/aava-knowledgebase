# GDPR — General Data Protection Regulation

## Overview
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Full Name | Regulation (EU) 2016/679 — General Data Protection Regulation |
| Effective Date | 25 May 2018 |
| Jurisdiction | EU/EEA; extraterritorial (applies to any org processing EU data subjects' data) |
| Enforcing Bodies | National Data Protection Authorities (DPAs): ICO (UK), CNIL (France), BfDI (Germany), etc. |
| Maximum Penalty | €20M or 4% of annual global turnover (whichever is higher) |
| Scope | Any processing of personal data of EU/EEA data subjects |

## Applicability to Banking
- Customer PII (name, address, DOB, email, phone, national ID)
- Transaction data linked to identifiable individuals
- Employee data
- Marketing and analytics data
- Third-party data sharing (payment processors, credit bureaus)

## Key Articles for Banking

| Article | Title | Priority | Banking Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Art. 5 | Principles of Processing | Critical | Data minimization, purpose limitation, storage limitation |
| Art. 6 | Lawfulness of Processing | Critical | Legal basis for every data processing activity |
| Art. 7 | Conditions for Consent | Critical | Marketing consent, analytics consent |
| Art. 13-14 | Information to Data Subjects | High | Privacy notices at point of collection |
| Art. 15 | Right of Access | High | Customer data portability |
| Art. 17 | Right to Erasure | Critical | Customer data deletion (with AML conflict) |
| Art. 25 | Data Protection by Design | Critical | Privacy-by-design in SDLC |
| Art. 30 | Records of Processing | Critical | Processing activity register |
| Art. 32 | Security of Processing | Critical | Encryption, access control, audit |
| Art. 33 | Breach Notification to Authority | Critical | 72-hour notification |
| Art. 34 | Breach Notification to Data Subjects | Critical | High-risk breach communication |
| Art. 35 | Data Protection Impact Assessment | High | DPIA for high-risk processing |
| Art. 44-49 | Cross-Border Transfers | Critical | SCCs, adequacy, BCRs |

## Conflicts with Other Regulations
| Conflict | Resolution |
|---|---|
| GDPR erasure vs AML 5-year retention | AML retention prevails for transaction records; anonymize customer reference; document legal basis per field |
| GDPR minimization vs KYC data collection | KYC collection justified under legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)); retain only what regulation requires |
| GDPR consent vs contractual necessity | Banking services: use contractual necessity (Art. 6(1)(b)) not consent for core services; consent only for optional processing |
