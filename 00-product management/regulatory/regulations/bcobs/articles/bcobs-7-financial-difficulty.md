# BCOBS 7 — Customers in Financial Difficulty

## Regulation Reference
- Sourcebook: BCOBS (FCA Handbook) — Chapter 7

## Obligation
Firms must treat customers in financial difficulty fairly and with forbearance. Firms must not pursue aggressive debt collection and must signpost customers to free debt advice.

## Technical Controls
1. Financial difficulty detection: missed payments, reduced income indicators, customer self-declaration
2. Forbearance options: payment holidays, reduced payments, fee waivers, breathing space
3. Debt advice signposting: automated signposting to free debt advice (StepChange, Citizens Advice, National Debtline)
4. Breathing Space: support Breathing Space scheme (60-day moratorium on enforcement)
5. Vulnerability identification: flag customers showing signs of vulnerability
6. Communication sensitivity: appropriate tone; no aggressive collection language
7. Charges review: consider waiving/reducing charges for customers in difficulty
8. Outcome monitoring: track outcomes for customers in financial difficulty

## Acceptance Criteria
Given a customer showing signs of financial difficulty, Then at least one forbearance option is offered AND free debt advice is signposted AND Breathing Space is supported if applicable AND charges are reviewed for fairness AND communications use appropriate, non-aggressive tone AND vulnerable customers receive enhanced support.
