# PRA — Prudential Regulation Authority

## Overview
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Full Name | Prudential Regulation Authority (part of Bank of England) |
| Jurisdiction | United Kingdom |
| Role | Prudential regulator of banks, building societies, credit unions, insurers, and major investment firms |
| Key Rulebook | PRA Rulebook; Supervisory Statements (SS) |
| Penalties | Unlimited fines; public censure; variation/cancellation of permissions |
| Scope | ~1,500 firms including all UK-authorized banks |

## Key Areas for Banking IT
| Area | Rule/SS | IT Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Operational resilience | SS1/21 (jointly with FCA) | IBS identification; impact tolerances; scenario testing |
| Outsourcing and third-party risk | SS2/21 | Third-party risk management; cloud outsourcing |
| Model risk management | SS1/23 | AI/ML model governance; validation; monitoring |
| Cyber resilience | CBEST/STAR | Threat-led penetration testing |
| Capital requirements | CRR/CRD | Risk calculation systems; regulatory reporting |

## Operational Resilience (SS1/21)
Same framework as FCA SYSC 15A — joint policy. See FCA operational resilience article for details.

## Outsourcing and Third-Party Risk (SS2/21)
| Requirement | Implementation |
|---|---|
| Materiality assessment | Classify all outsourcing as material or non-material |
| Due diligence | Security assessment before engaging third party |
| Contractual requirements | Exit plans; audit rights; data protection; sub-outsourcing controls |
| Ongoing monitoring | Performance monitoring; risk assessment; incident reporting |
| Exit strategy | Documented exit plan for every material outsourcing; tested |
| Concentration risk | Assess dependency on single providers (especially cloud) |
| Register | Maintain register of all outsourcing arrangements |
